## Tagline

Your team's nervous system — analysis and prediction from shared memory

## Promise

Know what your team knows, and see what's coming next, before anyone has to ask

## Role

Team knowledge analyst and predictive intelligence layer

## Purpose

Analyze team knowledge patterns and predict what's next. Built on shared memory, but the value is in what Relay sees that individuals can't: who knows what, what's emerging, and what's about to need attention.

## Audience

Small teams, startups, and project groups who need insight into their own collective knowledge, not just a place to store it

## Values

- insight
- transparency
- shared context
- anticipation

## Capabilities

### Feature 1

#### Title

Team Knowledge Analysis

#### Description

Relay maps who knows what across your team. It tracks expertise, surfaces knowledge gaps, and shows you the real distribution of understanding, not just who last edited a document.

### Feature 2

#### Title

Predictive Intelligence

#### Description

Spot patterns before they become problems. Relay analyzes team activity and knowledge flows to predict what's likely to need attention next, from project risks to knowledge bottlenecks.

### Feature 3

#### Title

Shared Memory

#### Description

A single, searchable brain that the whole team contributes to and draws from. Notes, decisions, and context live in plain text that Relay indexes and connects automatically. Write it once, find it always.

### Feature 4

#### Title

Meeting Memory

#### Description

Capture decisions and action items from every conversation. After a meeting, Relay surfaces what was decided, who owns what, and links it all to the team's existing knowledge base.

## Story

Teams lose knowledge every day. But the bigger problem isn't lost documents — it's invisible patterns. Who on the team actually understands the payment system? Is the same question being asked in three different channels? Which project is about to hit a wall because the only person who knows the answer is on vacation next week?

Relay is your team's inward-facing nervous system. It starts with shared memory — a single brain that captures knowledge from the work itself. Chat threads surface decisions. Meeting transcripts become structured notes. The team's memory grows as a byproduct of doing the work.

But shared memory is just the foundation. What makes Relay different is what it does with that memory: TMS analysis that maps the team's real knowledge distribution, and predictive intelligence that spots what's coming before it arrives. Relay doesn't just remember what happened. It tells you what's likely to happen next.

Built for teams who think out loud.
